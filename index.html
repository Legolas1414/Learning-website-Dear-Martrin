Dear Martin

Grade 9 English Project

About the Book

Dear Martin is a novel written by Nic Stone. It was published in 2017. The story follows Justice McAllister, a Black teenager living in the United States. Justice goes to an elite school with mostly white students. Even though he behaves well, he still experiences racism. The book shows his struggles with racism and Racial profiling, and how they affected him.

Main Message

The main message of Dear Martin is about how racism and racial profiling still exists. Racial profiling means people are judged because of the skin colorof their skin. The novel shows that Black people are often treated unfairly and not equal to Withe people. It also shows that even education and good behavior can`t always change that. Different characters react differently to racism. This makes the problem very clear for the reader.

Character Web

Manny is in the center of the character web because he plays an important role. He slowly realizes that racism and racial profiling still exist. Manny sees how unfair the system treats Black people. So he becomes more angry and frustrated over time. Manny is connected to many characters who show different opinions. And in the end, Manny becomes a victim of racial profiling himself.

Connection to Real Life

The problems shown in Dear Martin also exist in real life. Many Black people experience racism and police violence. Some are stopped by the police because of their skin color. And they are often treated unequal.

Martin Luther King Jr.

Martin Luther King Jr. was an important civil rights leader. He fought against racism using peaceful protests. Justice writes letters to him in the novel. Justice hopes to find answers through King’s ideas.
